Ghewar (घेवर)

    For the batter :

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter, solid form)
  • 1 cup cold water (add more as needed)
  • Ice cubes (to mix with ghee while rubbing)
  • Oil or ghee (for deep frying)

    For the sugar syrup :

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2–3 cardamom pods (crushed)
  • Few saffron strands (optional)

Steps to Make Ghewar

  1. Prepare the sugar syrup
    1. In a pan, add sugar + water.
    2. Boil until you get 1-string consistency (dip a spoon, press between fingers → it should form a single thread).
    3. Add cardamom & saffron, keep warm.
  2. Make the batter
    1. Take ghee in a bowl, add a few ice cubes, and rub until it turns white and fluffy.
    2. Add maida (flour) gradually, mixing well.
    3. Add cold water slowly, whisk until you get a smooth, flowing (runny) batter – thinner than pancake batter.
  3. Fry the Ghewar
    1. Heat ghee or oil in a deep kadhai (pan). Place a small steel ring mold in the center (optional but helps with shape).
    2. Pour the batter from a height (ladleful) into the hot ghee in the center of the pan.
    3. Let it spread & bubble – don’t stir. After it sets, add another ladleful in the center.
    4. Repeat 3–4 times until layers form & the Ghewar looks net-like.
    5. Fry until golden & crisp, then carefully lift out and place on a rack to drain excess oil.
  4. Soak & Garnish
    1. Dip the hot Ghewar briefly in warm sugar syrup, or drizzle syrup on top.
    2. Garnish with dry fruits & silver leaf.
